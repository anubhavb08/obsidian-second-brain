# Ikigai Pro - Industrial Buyer Growth Engine

This note captures the Ikigai Pro discussion that produced a client-facing business idea for a new personal growth venture, separate from Zlicc and separate from the existing partnership business.

## Personal context

- Current situation: already running a B2B partnership business, but exploring something owned independently.
- Near-term income goal: about USD 10K/month.
- Fundability: optional; possible investor connections exist if the idea becomes visibly profitable.
- Conflict boundary: avoid event-related and exhibition-related services as a business category. Exhibition support is allowed only as buyer activation around a manufacturing client's own participation, not as event/exhibition service delivery.

## Ikigai inputs

### Loves

- Learning about new AI tools.
- Experimenting deeply with generative and agentic AI tools.
- Coaching and training people.
- Creative AI work across image concepts, video generation, and visual storytelling.
- Stitching solutions from business problem statements.
- Business automation and marketing-function automation.

### Good at

- AI tool experimentation beyond passive content consumption.
- Paid AI films and complex solution videos.
- Simplifying complex ideas for non-technical audiences.
- Informal coaching/training that helps people understand tools and systems.
- Designing agentic workflows and a growth console with research, virtual CMO, contact discovery, campaign drafting, LinkedIn scanning, WhatsApp, email, and analytics agents.
- Technical product storytelling, including digital twin / XR-style machinery explanation work through the existing B2B company.

### Market needs observed

- AI adoption resistance inside companies.
- Teams stuck at chat-only AI rather than workflow automation.
- Repetitive work that remains manual and unstructured.
- Founder-led lead generation in under-resourced companies.
- Poor digital presence and inconsistent agency-led content for SMEs.
- Manufacturing SMEs wanting wider buyer reach, including export opportunities, but lacking digital discovery, RFQ readiness, and follow-up systems.

### Pays / proof

- Paid proof exists around AI creative films and solution videos.
- Technology consulting demand exists informally.
- Growth automation and AI workflow proof exists internally, not yet market-tested.
- Manufacturing-specific proof is early; the first commercial move should be pilot-first.

## Chosen niche

Family-led Indian manufacturing SMEs selling high-ticket machinery or industrial products, where buyer interest is scattered across offline channels, IndiaMART, TradeIndia, WhatsApp, weak websites, exhibitions, and founder relationships.

## Working offer

Industrial Buyer Growth Engine.

The offer helps manufacturing SMEs discover the right buyers, activate targeted campaigns, convert digital interest into RFQs, support exhibition build-up, and centralize every serious enquiry into a visible buyer pipeline.

## Client-facing modules

1. Buyer discovery
   - Use Apollo-like tools, LinkedIn, public web, directories, associations, marketplaces, and other compliant data sources.
   - Filter by industry, geography, company fit, plant size, revenue band, expansion signal, buyer role, and machinery need.

2. Targeted buyer campaigns
   - Turn discovered buyer lists into structured outreach.
   - Campaign channels can include email, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, and call-assist workflows depending on access and compliance.

3. Digital presence and RFQ conversion
   - Improve product pages and RFQ flows.
   - Make machinery pages buyer-readable with specifications, use cases, explainer assets, and enquiry prompts.

4. Exhibition build-up
   - Build a target-buyer list before a manufacturing client participates in an exhibition.
   - Run pre-show campaigns to drive qualified buyers to the client's booth.
   - Book meetings, share product previews, and manage post-show follow-up.
   - Boundary: this is buyer activation around exhibitions, not event or exhibition service delivery.

5. Pipeline console
   - Centralize source, lead quality, buyer fit, urgency, sales owner, next action, quote status, follow-up aging, and source-level ROI.

## Positioning

Not another posting agency.

Not a replacement for IndiaMART, TradeIndia, WhatsApp, LinkedIn, CRM tools, or existing team members.

The positioning is a central buyer growth operating layer that makes all channels work together and gives the founder visibility into serious buyer opportunities.

## Validation

Validation score from Ikigai Pro: 77/100.

Interpretation: solid enough to test. The main risk is not market pain; it is distribution and manufacturing-specific proof. The safest path is to sell a diagnostic sprint first, then convert early proof into a case study.

## First commercial motion

Start with a 14-day Pipeline Audit Sprint:

- Audit current lead sources, enquiry samples, product pages, marketplace usage, and sales follow-up process.
- Map target buyer segments, lead stages, scoring logic, and ownership rules.
- Create a first dashboard, RFQ readiness priorities, and target-account list.
- Recommend whether to move into a 90-day Buyer Growth Engine.
