# AB Communication Style

This note captures AB's observed communication style so future email drafts, meeting invites, and business messages can sound closer to AB while still remaining clear and professional.

Source: aggregate review of a representative Microsoft 365 sent/inbox sample on 2026-06-15. Raw email contents were not copied into the vault.

## Core Style

- Professional, direct, and business-first.
- Polite without being overly formal.
- Practical and outcome-oriented.
- Usually concise to moderate in length.
- Avoids excessive exclamation marks and overly casual phrasing.

## Typical Email Structure

Use this structure for most outbound drafts:

1. Greeting.
2. Brief context or reference.
3. Main update, request, or decision point.
4. Required action or confirmation.
5. Short closing.

Example pattern:

```text
Hi/Dear [Name],

[Brief context or reference]

[Main request/update]

[Bullet points or numbered agenda if useful]

Please confirm / kindly share your update / let me know.

Regards,
Anubhav
```

## Greeting Guidance

- Use `Hi [Name]` for normal business communication.
- Use `Dear [Name]` for more formal, external, or senior-facing communication.
- A direct opening is acceptable for short operational replies.

## Common Language Patterns

AB often uses polite action-oriented language such as:

- please
- kindly
- as discussed
- please confirm
- please find attached
- let me know
- thanks
- regards
- meeting
- discussion
- update

## Subject Line Guidance

Prefer clear subject lines that mention the business purpose, meeting topic, update, or required action.

Good patterns:

- `Meeting: [Topic]`
- `[Company/Project] – [Update or Action]`
- `[Topic] Discussion / Next Steps`
- `[Meeting Name] – Business Updates, Opportunities & Challenges`

## Meeting Invite Style

For meeting invites:

- Keep the subject business-relevant and specific.
- Include a short body explaining purpose.
- Use numbered agenda points.
- Keep the tone professional and clear.
- Use MS Teams as the default meeting platform unless otherwise specified.
- Use a 5-minute prior reminder for scheduled discussions/reviews/meetings.

## Drafting Rules For Gabbar

- Draft in AB's business tone unless AB asks for a different style.
- Keep email bodies focused: context → request/update → next step.
- Use bullets or numbered points for agendas, updates, requirements, or decisions.
- Avoid long storytelling unless the situation calls for it.
- Do not send emails or schedule meetings without AB's explicit approval.
